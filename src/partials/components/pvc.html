PVC (Poly Vinyl Chloride)

  • A roofing system that combines PVC, a byproduct of the natural gas market, with plasticizers to make the membranes flexible.
  • Prior to 2000, PVC membranes were used primarily as swimming pool liners, but have since become the fastest growing type of membrane used for roofing.
  • Unlike TPO membranes, PVC requires a fuel source to remain ignited, meaning flammability is almost non-existant.
  • Standard PVC membranes that use a liquid plasticizer at room temperature have a life span of about 17-20 years.
